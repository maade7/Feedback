About Feedback

Getting leftover food to where it’s needed most.

Feedback was designed to combat two major problems: food waste and hunger. Feedback is a new platform connecting the food service businesses that often experience a surplus of leftovers with the organizations helping to get food to the people who need it most. Feedback removes the major hurdles preventing donations and simplifies the process to eliminate food waste and hunger in the US.




The Process is this Simple

Step One:

A food service business lists available food on the feedback platform.

Step Two:

A non-profit organization identifies a donation through the app that meets their needs and claims it.

Step Three:

That organization's designated driver picks up the donation adn delivers it to their location.

Step Four:

The organization distributes the donation to those in need.

Why food waste is one of the dumbest problems we face.

Every year the average American family throws out more than $2,000 in food. 30-40% of food produced domestically ends up in the trash. When that food ends up in landfills it produces greenhouse gases that are more potent than carbon dioxide. When it comes to the largest producers of greenhouse gases, food waste produced emissions would rank #3, behind only China and the US.

Stats aside, what makes this problem so dumb is that there are millions of people going hungry while these leftovers are left to rot. By working to connect the food waste with the people who need it we are working to help the hunger and also help the planet.

How it works

How to donate and get food using our app.

Donate Food

This is where it all starts and where the biggest hurdles lie because without donations, we don’t have anything. We’ve designed our platform to be as simple and intuitive as possible so that you can post your food quickly and get back to the myriad of other things you need to do.

  • Take a photo and provide detail/description to post your food donation
  • Get notified when someone claims your food and track the driver real-time on navigation
  • Your donation is picked up safely

Find and Receive Food

Your organization is busy and tracking down food is not something you have time for. That’s why we’ve made our ever-changing database as simple to use as possible, or you can even set up notifications so that whenever a posting fits your designated parameters you’ll automatically be notified.

  • Search or browse for food by distance, type, quantity, etc.
  • Once you find the type of food that you need, claim it and set the ETA to inform the donor
  • Drive to the location and pick up your food

Access Streamlined Tax Deductions

Feedback’s automated tax deductions feature helps your business unlock tens of thousands of dollars in additional financial savings. We keep track of all your donations and with the click of a button you can access all the documentation you’ll need for your taxes.

Frequently Asked Questions

You’ve got questions. We’ve got the answers. Here are a couple of the ones we get asked most often, but if you have something else you’re curious about please don’t hesitate to reach out. We’re passionate about Feedback and we want you to be as well.

  • What cities does Feedback currently operate in?

    Feedback is based in Atlanta and currently operates only here. We have identified the next group of cities that we’ll be expanding to and look forward to launching in these in 2022 and continuing to grow the Feedback family.

  • How do I receive tax benefits?

    When you login to the app you will be able to access a downloadable copy of your tax receipt from your user profile. This will outline your donations for the year. Include this in your yearly tax return and you will be able to claim a deduction for your donations.

  • How can I help pick up and deliver food to those in need?

    Our non-profit partners are always in need of help picking up and delivering food. You can find a local orgranization through the app. Once you find one, or several, that you'd like to help please contact them directly and they will give you an access code to use when you set up an account that will verify your approval to pick up food for their organization.

  • Are there any legal issues that I need to be aware of when donating food?

    The Bill Emerson Good Samaritan Food Donation Act of 1996 encourages the donation of food and grocery products to nonprofit organizations for distribution to needy individuals. The Act exempts “persons and gleaners” who make good faith donations of food to nonprofit organizations that feed the hungry from liability for injuries arising from the consumption of the donated food.

  • What does my organization need to do to become a recipient of donations?

    All you have to do is create a recipient account with the name of the organization that is on file with the IRS. We will verify that your organization qualifies within 72 hours and will then activate your account to start claiming donations.
